My first experience with VR was Skyrim. I had played briefly with a friends HTC Vive. I enjoyed my time on the game so I decided to toy around with it a bit. I found that odd movements can easily cause a state of vertigo. Mounts were one of these things. So was falling of a cliff.(Stupid Giant- The jerk smacked me right off a cliff and I practically let him.) The controller I was using along with anything rapidly approaching my field of vision could scare the crap out of me if I was unprepared.(Again stupid giant). I did not get motion sickness like some people do. It was a feeling of Vertigo that could easily be mistaken as excitement. One con is that if anything is poorly drawn in the game it became much more vivid. Skyrim had a few crummy character textures that may make it less realistic up close. Another issue is that I did experience some stuttering. I would guess this is because the graphics card at the time had to render for a stereoscopic point of view rather then just a monitor. The pros are that I was less distracted from anything outside. I also didn’t strain my neck as much because I could just lean back with my controller. I would however would never try to compare it with full dive VR. It’s more like ocular headphones. While in ear headphones eliminate the need for speakers giving you an almost surround sound like effect. The VR headset had quite the same effect. In any case just as I prefer headphones over speakers I also prefer the HTC Vive over a monitor

sandy sallisa
